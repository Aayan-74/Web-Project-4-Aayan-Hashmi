About Us

A Vision Rooted in Tradition

Naawi-Oodena, meaning “centre of the heart and community,” is more than a development—it is a bold step toward Indigenous self-determination, reconciliation, and community-building. With the help of the Treaty One Nations and Canada Lands Company this urban reserve has been developed on the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg, Manitoba, and was a collaborative effort to create a space that honors Indigenous culture and history.

Our Mission

Naawi-Oodena is committed to creating a vibrant, sustainable community that honors the land and its history. We aim to foster economic growth, cultural revitalization, and social well-being for all.

Who's Involved?

The 2 main partners involved in the development of Naawi-Oodena are Canada Lands Company CLC limited and the Treaty One Nations. The seven Treaty One First Nations include: Peguis First Nation, Long Plain First Nation, Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Roseau River Anishinabe First Nation, Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation, Swan Lake First Nation, and Sagkeeng First Nation.
