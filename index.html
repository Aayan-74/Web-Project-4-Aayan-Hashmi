What is Naawi-Oodena?

Naawi-Oodena is the name given to the redevelopment of the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg. It is a 160-acre urban reserve being jointly developed by the Treaty One Nations and the Canada Lands Company. Naawi-Oodena is a visionary new urban reserve being developed in Winnipeg by Treaty One Nations and Canada Lands Company. Rooted in Indigenous culture and guided by the values of sustainability, inclusion, and connection, it will be home to thousands in the future. Naawi-Oodena will be a place of gathering, a place of healing, and a place of learning. It will be a place where the past meets the present and the future. Naawi-Oodena will be a place where the land and the people are connected and it will be a place where the stories of the land and the people are shared. It will be a place where the land and the people are respected and a place where the land and the people are cared for.

What are going to be the features in this community

In the plan of Naawi-Oodena there are going to be many features that will be included in the community. The plan includes: A central ceremonial site Cultural pavilions and gathering spaces An Indigenous cultural centre Open parks and plazas are designed to support community events and storytelling. Trails and pedestrian pathways Playgrounds and sports fields Spaces for seasonal activities like powwows, markets, and festivals. The plan proposes a diverse range of housing including: Multi-unit residential buildings (apartments, townhomes) Affordable and market-rate housing Opportunities for intergenerational living and Elders housing.
